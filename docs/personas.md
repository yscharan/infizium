# Personas

_Last updated: 2026-06-07_

These are the four people Infizium is built for. Every feature decision is filtered through one of these four lives.

---

## 1. Lakshmi — Parent, 38, Mother of Two

**City:** Hyderabad (Kukatpally area)
**School:** St. Joseph's High School — private English medium
**Children:** Arjun (Grade 9) and a daughter (Grade 6)
**Occupation:** Home tailor — works from 8 AM to 6 PM
**Device:** Android phone, WhatsApp, occasionally uses YouTube
**Language:** Telugu primary, understands basic English
**Income:** ₹15,000–18,000/month household

### Background
Lakshmi runs a small tailoring business from home. She is ambitious for her children but has no time to visit school. She checks WhatsApp constantly between work — it is her lifeline to the outside world. She does not want to download another app. She has tried school apps before and stopped using them after the first week.

Her husband works in construction and is away from home most of the week. All school decisions — fees, trips, PTMs — fall on her. She attends PTM twice a year and feels the gap: the school knows her children, but she only gets information twice a year.

She watches Arjun leave at 7:30 AM every morning and spends the next 20 minutes wondering if he got on the bus. She has no way to know until he calls from school — which he rarely does.

### Goals
- Know her children reached school safely, every single day
- Be consulted before anything important is decided about her child
- See homework so she can remind the children to complete it
- Approve or reject school trips without taking a half-day off work
- Not be flooded with irrelevant notifications from a 200-member WhatsApp group
- Understand the sponsorship option — her daughter might benefit from it

### Pain Points
- Currently gets information only at PTMs (twice a year)
- Misses critical notices buried in the school WhatsApp group of 200 parents
- Has to take time off work to sign forms at school in person
- No visibility into homework or test schedules
- Cannot afford for either child to miss school — every day counts
- Feels like the school doesn't respect her time

### How Lakshmi Uses Infizium

**Daily (WhatsApp — she never opens the app):**
- 7:51 AM: Gets a WhatsApp message — "✅ Arjun boarded Bus #4. ETA school: 19 min."
- 7:51 AM: "🏫 Arjun reached school. Good morning!"
- If absent: "❌ Arjun was marked absent today. Reply with reason or call school: 040-XXXXXXX"

**Weekly:**
- Friday evening: Gets a WhatsApp summary — attendance %, pending homework, upcoming tests

**For approvals:**
- Receives: "🏕️ Field trip to Nehru Zoo on June 12. Cost: ₹150. Reply YES to approve, NO to decline."
- Replies "YES" — done. No visit to school required.

**For questions (AI bot):**
- Texts "Arjun attendance ela undi?" (How's Arjun's attendance?)
- Gets: "✅ Arjun attended 24 of 28 days this month — 86%. He was absent on June 4."
- Texts "homework enti undi" (What homework is there?)
- Gets: "📚 2 assignments due: Maths (Chapter 7, due Jun 9) and Physics (Chapter 5 numericals, due Jun 10)"

**Commute (when live):**
- Smart Band on Arjun's wrist — she sees him leave, board the bus, arrive at school
- Gets three WhatsApp pings: left home, boarded bus, reached school
- SOS button on the band: if Arjun presses it, she gets a live location link immediately

**Permissions:**
- She controls everything from a permissions screen (or by replying to a WhatsApp menu)
- She can turn off AI tutor, turn off location tracking, opt out of specific notification types
- All governed by DPDP — school cannot override her choices

## 2. Ravi — Teacher, 34, Grade 8 Class Teacher

**City:** Hyderabad
**Subject:** Mathematics (teaches Grade 8 and 9)
**Class:** Class teacher for Grade 8A — 42 students
**Experience:** 12 years
**Device:** Android, WhatsApp, uses Excel for marks
**Language:** Telugu and English

### Background
Ravi is a dedicated teacher who genuinely wants to help students improve. He has been teaching for 12 years and knows which students are falling behind before they do. But his time is consumed by non-teaching work: attendance registers, WhatsApp replies from parents at 10 PM, marking count sheets, and preparing compliance reports.

He currently manages a class WhatsApp group with all parents. Every message he sends goes to all 42 parents. Parents reply at all hours. He has no boundary. He answers because he cares, but it's burning him out.

He marks attendance in a physical register at the start of every period. On a bad day, the register is on his desk when class starts in another room. On a good day, it takes four minutes. Across six periods, that's 24 minutes per day just on attendance.

### Goals
- Mark attendance quickly at the start of each period — under 90 seconds
- Identify struggling students early — before the terminal exam, not after
- Communicate with parents without it becoming a second job
- Give meaningful homework feedback without spending hours correcting
- Keep student progress data in one place instead of spreadsheets

### Pain Points
- Attendance register is a physical book — gets lost, not backed up
- Parent messages arrive at all hours with no structure or boundaries
- No easy way to track which students are consistently struggling across subjects
- Has to manually compile attendance data for admin every month
- Parents call him instead of the school for information he doesn't have

### How Ravi Uses Infizium

**Every morning (Teacher dashboard):**
- Opens the teacher dashboard — sees "Grade 8A: 38 present, 4 absent as of 8:05 AM"
- Marks attendance from mobile in the classroom — tap Present/Absent per student
- System auto-sends WhatsApp alerts to absent students' parents — Ravi doesn't do it manually

**Posting homework:**
- Creates an assignment: Subject "Maths", Title "Chapter 7 — Fractions", Due Date: June 10
- All Grade 8A parents get a WhatsApp message automatically
- Students see it in their feed

**Tracking progress:**
- Dashboard shows him which students have been absent more than 3 times this month
- (Phase 3) Early warning flags — "Ananya's Maths score dropped 18% in 3 weeks"
- He acts before the terminal exam, not after

**Parent communication:**
- Sends updates through Infizium — not his personal WhatsApp
- Parents can only message him through the platform during school hours
- After 6 PM, messages are held and delivered next morning

**For teachers of Ravi's type, the promise is:**
"You teach. We handle the admin. You get your evenings back."

## 3. Arjun — Student, 15, Grade 9

**City:** Hyderabad (Kukatpally)
**School:** St. Joseph's High School
**Grade:** 9A
**Strengths:** Maths, Physics
**Struggles:** English writing, History essays
**Aspiration:** Crack JEE and become an engineer
**Device:** Occasional access to his mother's phone, school computer lab
**Language:** Telugu at home, English at school

### Background
Arjun is bright and ambitious but operates in a system that doesn't give him feedback in time to act on it. He gets a report card twice a year. In between, he has no idea if he is on track. He re-reads notes before exams because no one has taught him how to study differently.

He forgets homework that was given verbally in class. He is too shy to ask questions in a 42-student classroom. When he has doubts at 9 PM, there is no one to ask except YouTube.

His commute is 22 minutes on Bus #4. He boards at Kukatpally Bus Stand. He never thinks about how his mother worries during those 22 minutes.

### Goals
- Know what homework is due and when — not find out the day it's due
- Understand concepts, not just pass tests
- Get feedback that helps him improve, not just a mark
- Know how he is doing without waiting for report cards
- Have someone (or something) to ask questions to at night

### Pain Points
- Forgets homework assignments given verbally
- Doubts sit unanswered until the next class, two days later
- Doesn't know what to study — just re-reads notes
- Feels anxious before exams because his progress is invisible to him
- Can't afford extra tuition — family budget doesn't allow it

### How Arjun Uses Infizium

**Homework feed (dashboard):**
- Sees all pending assignments with due dates — no more "I forgot"
- Gets a reminder the evening before a due date (Phase 2)

**AI Tutor (Phase 3):**
- Types "I don't understand how to solve quadratic equations" at 9:30 PM
- Gets a worked example in simple English, with a follow-up practice question
- Asks again in Telugu if he's confused — tutor responds in Telugu
- AI knows he's weak in English writing (from teacher's data) and adjusts how it explains things

**Progress dashboard:**
- Sees his own attendance %, subject scores, trend arrows (up/down from last month)
- JEE Readiness Index — calculated from current performance across subjects
- Exam countdown timer — "12 days to terminal exam"

**Commute (when Smart Band is live):**
- Wears the Infizium Smart Band — looks like a watch
- Doesn't have to do anything — band auto-activates commute mode at 7:25 AM
- If something goes wrong, holds the SOS button for 3 seconds
- His mother knows he's safe. He doesn't have to text her.

**For students of Arjun's type, the promise is:**
"You won't fall behind in silence anymore. We'll tell you — and your teacher — before it's too late."

## 4. Priya — School Administrator, 48, Vice Principal

**City:** Hyderabad
**School:** St. Joseph's High School — 800 students, ~40 teachers
**Role:** Vice Principal — day-to-day operations, parent relations, compliance
**Reports to:** Principal and School Trust
**Device:** Desktop + Android phone
**Language:** Telugu and English
**Experience:** 22 years in education

### Background
Priya has seen every school management system come and go. She is skeptical of new tools. Her last attempt at a "digital school" involved a software vendor who charged ₹3 lakhs, delivered a broken app, and disappeared. She is now running everything on WhatsApp groups, paper files, and a shared Excel sheet.

She starts every morning calling three class teachers to get a rough attendance count. She sends announcements to a 200-member WhatsApp group and has no idea who read them. When the school trust asks for an attendance report, she builds it manually from 40 teachers' registers.

She is not anti-technology. She is anti-wasted time. If a tool saves her real time, she will use it.

### Goals
- See school-wide attendance in real time without calling each class teacher
- Send circulars to all parents instantly with a delivery and read record
- Collect signed consent forms without chasing parents for weeks
- Generate board-ready reports without building Excel files manually
- Have an audit trail when parents claim they never received a notice
- Build trust with parents as a transparent, accountable institution

### Pain Points
- No single real-time view of school-wide attendance or performance
- Parent complaints arrive without context or history — "my child was marked absent but she was there"
- No audit trail for notices — parents say they never received them
- Fee reminders require calling each parent individually
- 40 teachers giving her attendance data in 40 different formats

### How Priya Uses Infizium

**Every morning (Admin dashboard):**
- Opens admin dashboard at 8:15 AM — sees "312 present, 488 absent as of 8:10 AM" across all classes
- Sees each class with a colour-coded attendance bar — green (≥90%), amber (≥80%), red (<80%)
- Grade 9B at 73% — calls that class teacher directly, informed

**Announcements:**
- Types announcement: "School closed June 9 — State board inspection"
- Selects "Send via WhatsApp to all parents"
- 780 parents receive it in under 60 seconds
- Dashboard shows: 612 read, 168 not yet read — she can follow up on the 168

**Forms and consent:**
- Creates form: "Science Field Trip — Nehru Zoological Park — June 15. Cost: ₹150. Parent approval required."
- Form sent via WhatsApp to all Grade 8 parents
- Dashboard: 287 approved, 25 declined, 38 pending
- One-click reminder to the 38 pending parents
- DPDP-compliant audit log — "Lakshmi Devi approved at 9:47 AM on June 3"

**Board reporting (Phase 2):**
- Clicks "Generate Report" — gets a PDF with attendance by class, announcement reach, form response rates
- Presents to school trust without building a single Excel file

**For admins of Priya's type, the promise is:**
"You'll spend 40 minutes less on admin every morning. And when a parent says 'I never got that notice,' you'll have the proof."

## Persona Interaction Map

How the four personas interact with each other through Infizium:

```
PRIYA (Admin)
  ├── Creates announcements → LAKSHMI receives on WhatsApp
  ├── Creates consent forms → LAKSHMI approves on WhatsApp
  ├── Sees ARJUN's attendance in school-wide dashboard
  └── Grants RAVI access to his class data

RAVI (Teacher)
  ├── Marks attendance → LAKSHMI receives alert → ARJUN's record updated
  ├── Posts homework → ARJUN sees in feed → LAKSHMI notified on WhatsApp
  └── Gets early warning about ARJUN's performance drop (Phase 3)

LAKSHMI (Parent)
  ├── Approves field trip → PRIYA's form shows approval
  ├── Asks AI bot questions → MCP tools query ARJUN's real data
  ├── Tracks ARJUN's commute → Smart Band sends location to her WhatsApp
  └── Controls all data permissions for both her children

ARJUN (Student)
  ├── Sees homework feed created by RAVI
  ├── AI tutor answers his questions at night (Phase 3)
  ├── His commute is visible to LAKSHMI via Smart Band
  └── Can receive sponsorship support (anonymous donor pays his fees)
```

## Persona Priority for Build Order

When two features compete for dev time, build for this priority:

1. **Lakshmi first** — if she can't use it on WhatsApp without the app, it doesn't ship
2. **Priya second** — admin trust is the sales cycle (she decides if the school adopts Infizium)
3. **Ravi third** — teacher adoption drives data quality (garbage attendance in = garbage alerts out)
4. **Arjun fourth** — student features are Phase 2+ (students don't have buying power or decision-making authority)
